Introduction and Objective

Within the scope of this study, my goal is to identify which analytical skills are now in high demand in the labour market. By analysing LinkedIn job postings, I hope to provide job seekers with valuable insights into the skills required for success in analytics careers.

Methodology

Specifically, I scraped LinkedIn for Data Scientist and Data Analyst job listings using Python and the BeautifulSoup library. I zeroed in on strategic areas in Vietnam and India to find the best job openings. Following this, the data was processed and cleaned before being analysed in the Jupyter Lab.

Analysis

Data Collection

This investigation relied on information mined from the professional networking site LinkedIn. I gathered data on two overarching role categories: üöÄ Data Scientist and üìä Data Analyst. My scheme is based on fetching a job description, returned by GET method to check skills in demand: Python, Power BI, Jupyter, SQL, Tableau, Excel. Noting that I use publicly accessible LinkedIn data for purely academic and instructional reasons.

BeautifulSoup is being used to extract data from online job boards. The web pages are retrieved using the requests library, and then information like job positions and necessary abilities are extracted using the soup method.

Data Integrity

We subjected the collected data to stringent reviews and validation procedures to ensure accuracy and integrity. Any biases caused by site scraping were accounted for, and we dealt with any missing or inconsistent data points appropriately.

Data Visualization

For a comprehensive grasp, I built interactive and aesthetically appealing visualisations in two distinct ways: by continuing the analysis in Jupyter and by exporting the database to Power BI. Multiple graph styles were employed to illustrate the correlation between skill categories, levels of expertise, and the prevalence of job postings.

Delhi has emerged as the city with the highest number of job postings (43.27%) in India, while Ho Chi Minh City takes the lead in Vietnam with 55.75%.
